GTF Grounding Tracker

Tracking the Pratt & Whitney GTF crisis — supply chain drag, fleet impact, and recovery signals

Last updated: January 01, 2026

As of October 2025, 835 GTF-powered aircraft (34.1% of the global GTF fleet) remain on the ground awaiting Pratt & Whitney PW1000G engine inspections or repair — up from 687 (29.7%) in October 2024, a net increase of 148 aircraft. At the airline level, VivaAerobus is the hardest hit with 47.5% of its GTF fleet grounded. On the supply-chain side, P&W MRO throughput has improved — the internal output index rose from 100 in 2024 to an estimated 139 by Q4 2025 — yet the grounded count continues to climb, reflecting ongoing delivery of new aircraft that themselves require inspection before entering service. The data suggests the crisis is not yet over.
